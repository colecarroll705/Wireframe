Frequently Asked Questions

Question One: How does an Etch-A-Sketch work?

An Etch-A-Sketch works using a mechanical stylus inside the screen that scrapes aluminum powder off the back of the display. As the stylus moves, it creates dark lines that form your drawing.

Question Two: Why do faint lines remain after erasing?

If faint lines remain, the powder may not be evenly spread. Shaking the Etch-A-Sketch a little more usually redistributes the powder and clears the screen completely.

Question Three: Why is it hard to draw curves or diagonal lines?

Curves and diagonal lines require turning both knobs at the same time. This takes practice because the knobs must be moved carefully and in coordination.

Question Four: Why can I only draw lines instead of lifting the stylus?

The stylus inside the Etch-A-Sketch stays in contact with the screen at all times. Because of this, the drawing is made from one continuous line rather than separate strokes.
